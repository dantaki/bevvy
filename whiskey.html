Old Fashioned

Muddle the sugar cube and bitters in an Old-Fashioned glass. Add the bourbon and 1 large ice cube and stir until chilled. Garnish with the lemon and orange twist. First squeeze or express the orange twist over the cocktail, and then rub it gently around the rim of the glass. Only twist the lemon peel over the cocktail, do not rub it around the glass. Brown sugar adds a more rustic and authentic taste, assuming people didn't have white sugar in the 1800s. In either case, brown sugar imparts a molasses flavor that pairs well. Sometimes I use orange bitters with Angostura to lift the orange profile of the cocktail. Usually in a 2:1 ratio of Angostura to orange bitters.
1 sugar cube is equal to 1 teaspoon. A dash is somewhere in between 1/4 and 1/8 teaspoon.

  • 2oz Bourbon
  • 2 Dashes Angostura Bitters
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 1 Lemon twist and 1 orange twist

Manhattan

Traditionally made with rye whiskey, bourbon Manhattans are just as good and more spicy than the standard rye whiskey.

  • 2oz Whiskey
  • 1oz Sweet Vermouth
  • Dash Angostura Bitters
  • Add a Luxardo Cherry

Improved Whiskey Cocktail

Taken from the Cocktail Codex, this cocktail tastes very much like a Sazerac but is made with Bourbon. Express the lemon peel over the cocktail, do not rub it around the glass. Very tasty, wife really enjoyed this one.

  • 2oz Bourbon
  • 1 Tsp Maraschino liqueur
  • 1/8 Tsp Absinthe
  • 1/8 Tsp Angostura Bitters
  • 1/8 Tsp Peychaud's Bitters
  • Garnish with a lemon twist

Sazerac

From Scrappy's Bitters, stir and strain into a chilled glass with an absinthe rinse. Garnish with a lemon peel. Note 3-4 dashes is equal to 1 dropper.

  • 3oz Rye
  • 3/4 oz Simple syrup
  • 3-4 Dashes Scrappy's Orleans Bitters
  • Garnish with a lemon twist

Flatbush

Variation of the Brooklyn from page 87 of the Cocktail Codex. Since Bigallet China-China Amer is not something I keep stocked in the house. It's a "orange-based, bittersweet liqueur peppered with botanical infusions... anise, spicy clove" - per the distiller's notes. Substitute with a healthy dose of Angostura and Orange Bitters

  • 2oz Rye
  • 3/4 oz Dry vermouth
  • 1 Tsp Maraschino liqueur
  • 1/2 Tsp Angostura Bitters
  • 1/2 Tsp Orange Bitters
  • Garnish with a brandied cherry
